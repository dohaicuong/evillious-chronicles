In the middle of a barren field, Irina once more opened her eyes.

The pain in her body had all but vanished.

“This is…”

She could talk.

Her field of vision was satisfactory…But strangely, it felt to her as though the surface of the ground was closer than it had been before.

“I’ve gotten shorter—no, that’s not it.”

Her limbs, and her body.

Everything was smaller than it had been, and was covered in red fur.

It took pretty much no time at all to grasp the situation.

Irina—had become a cat.

Her soul had been put inside the cat device.

“Excellent…the activation was successful.”

The man who must have done this to her nodded in satisfaction.

His body looked tattered.

Despite the state of him, rather than tend to his wounds, Seth had…prioritized his “experiment”.

…To save me?

That probably hadn’t been his intention.

Even so, she still said to him, “…Thank you.”

Seth turned his back to her, not responding to her words. “…I won’t return here again. My ‘experiment’ is over. But you…You should continue your own ‘experiment’.”

“My…’experiment’?”

“What you will do now…and how you will live, these are things you must decide yourself.”

Seth started to walk, dragging his body along.

Heading south.

The same direction that light fell before.

There must have been something there that Seth was after.

Lu li la.

The song she’d heard somewhere.

It continued to resound through Irina’s body.
