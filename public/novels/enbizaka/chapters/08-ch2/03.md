Susa, Enkoku, Amayomi; they traveled through many lands, and as they did so Gakusha learned the way of the enlightened one’s teachings from Giyara.

And so, after a span of four years had passed, Gakusha became a full-fledged monk.

.

Around the time that they had done a full lap around Jakoku and arrived in a village called Momogengou.

Giyara fell gravely ill.

According to the doctors, he likely didn’t have much longer to live.

“I suppose the time has come for me to say farewell to this transient world.”

Giyara appeared to have made his resolve, but Gakusha continued to weep by his bedside.

“Teacher. I still have so much I want to learn from you. Please don’t die.”

“I must. Death visits all men equally. There is no way for me alone to run from it.”

“But—”

“Gakusha. In these four years you have grown into a splendid monk. I worry for you, that you have not yet regained your memory…But I think that you will do fine, even when I am gone.”

“…”

“You may continue to study the enlightened teachings, or you could search for a new way of living…Do as you would like to do.”

With those as Giyara’s parting words, he closed his eyes.

And he never opened them again.
