Kokutan-douji finished his goodbye with his friends, and soon enough night had fallen.

A beautiful full moon hung in the night sky.

“Well then—Shall we get going?” Bufuko—no, Behemo—said, floating his body up into the air.

And then, Kokutan-douji similarly flew up.

“Are you ready too, Kagura-san?” Behemo asked.

–Yeah, don’t worry, I replied, once more thinking about Kayo.

Sin was sin.

Punishment was punishment.

What she had done could never be forgiven.

For it was true that she had killed as far as Mei, Miku, and Rin for the sake of her own envy and revenge.

Even so.

Enbizaka lay spread out before me.

I knew that Kayo had wanted to protect this place.

And that she had.

I wish.

Even if her soul has fallen to hell.

I can only pray for her to have at least a little happiness.

With Behemo and Kokutan-douji, I soared high into the air.
Our destination, paradise.

The Heavenly Yard.

That was.

In the living world—

.

The place called the “Moon”.
