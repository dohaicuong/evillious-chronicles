Once the sun had gone down, night fell as was natural.

That day was one with a pretty, full moon.

…Thinking on it now, every time Kayo killed someone, it was always at times like that when a round moon gleamed in the night sky.

Kokutan-douji had gone out, and was yet to come back.

Him going out to stay somewhere like that was in itself not terribly uncommon. Douji would occasionally do things like search for clues on the murders with his friends, or assist Saruteito with her search for the blades, and as such would sometimes not be back until morning.
But if he had come back to the tailor shop on time on that day—on that day alone, fate might have yet been changed.

Be that as it may, it was already too late. Douji did not come home, and on that night Kayo was at the tailor shop by herself…There was no one who could have stopped her.

“…Come, let’s begin tailoring.”

Kayo smiled to herself.

The only person who saw that wicked smile, one that could not possibly be imagined from seeing her in the daytime—was myself, without a human form.

.

And so, Kayo began her third—“nighttime tailoring”.
