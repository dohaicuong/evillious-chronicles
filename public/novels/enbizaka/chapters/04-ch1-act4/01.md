As far as I know, excepting the time of the great fire it had been over ten years since a murder had occurred in Enbizaka.

After receiving the news, the private detective Eikichi immediately rushed to the scene.

When he had managed to push through the curious onlookers, he found that constable Uibee was already there, carrying his favored truncheon.

Upon seeing Eikichi arrive, Uibee wordlessly lifted up a woven mat made of straw that was there.

Lying under it was a naked woman’s corpse, her neck brutally slashed.

“…Whattan awful sight.”

Eikichi put his hand near the corpse, closing his eyes.

“Her throat’s been slit with a blade. And there’s a stab wound in the chest besides…That’s probably the fatal injury,” Uibee explained, regarding the corpse.

“A blade…You think the offender’s a samurai?”

“No, I don’t think she was killed by somethin’ sharp like a katana. It was somethin’ else.”

“What do you think it is?”

“I dunno yet. But given she was dumped here with her kimono taken off, it was probably the work of a passing mugger.”

“Do we know who the woman was?”

“Yeah. While you were still snoozin’ I went ahead and looked it all up for ya. …This is the wife of the Miroku shop owner.”
