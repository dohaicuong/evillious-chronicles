Though I’ve slipped out of the fire, I can still feel the heat remaining on my body.

…Father, huh.

I also never knew my father.

Though my situation’s a bit more severe than Nemesis’ case.

Since she was young my mother’s spent her time going through various men. One of them was probably my father, but unless I could test all of them I would never be able to determine which one it was. Maybe my mother didn’t even know.

It isn’t that my mother—Rahab Barisol—is a prostitute or anything. She’s a respectable scientist, and the vice director of this very clinic.

People who work in socially respectable jobs aren’t always upright and dependable people in their personal lives. Thanks to those closest to me I have become quite painfully aware of this fact.

.

The memory flames have long past extinguished, leaving only charred rubble behind.

But it seems there’s still a memory I’ve yet to see.
