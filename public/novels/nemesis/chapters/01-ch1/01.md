People’s memories are not tidily arranged like books shelved in a book-case.

Their order is all over the place. Sometimes they’re fuzzy, and some are omitted completely.

To get them set up neatly would take way too much time, and frankly it’d be a fruitless task.

So—picking out the newest and clearest memories first is standard theory for the ‘Swap’.

I search for something suitable as I gaze upon the sea of memories.

This one looks good—I think that to myself as I pick up this recollection.

Depicted in it I can see…An elderly black man.
