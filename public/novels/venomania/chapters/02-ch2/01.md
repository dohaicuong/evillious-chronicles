“It’s best if we don’t see each other again,“ the woman said.

“What, why,” the man questioned closely.

“Just because,” she replied.

“I can’t accept that.” The man refused to budge.

The woman’s miserable face turned into a sullen expression.

Then, the woman said.

“How irritating.”

“Why are you so obtuse,” she continued.

“I didn’t actually like you from the start,” she continued.

“I feel like puking just by being beside you,” she continued.

“It’s awfully unbearable,” she continued.

Finally, the woman said with a warped expression.

“Don’t come near me with that ugly face.”

I’m not ugly.

I’m not the ugly one.

Your face right now is what’s ugly.

And then, the man awoke.
