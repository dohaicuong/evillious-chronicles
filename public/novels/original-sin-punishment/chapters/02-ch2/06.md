…As she became aware of her body falling, at the same time Ly knew that she’d been defeated.

Where did I go wrong?

It was much too late for any regrets.

She would never be graced with the chance to make use of her failure next time.

Ly’s hands were still gripping the twin blades.

Maybe she could stop her descent by stabbing them into something.

…It’s no use. I’d never be able to reach.

She could hear the sound of water flowing below her.

Ly knew that there was no sea at the bottom of this cliff.

It was a river.

A great river that flowed into the forest of Held.

If I fall in there, maybe—

It was a slim hope. But it was all she had.

She twisted her body with all her might. She didn’t know if she could change the trajectory of her fall by doing so, but regardless there wasn’t anything else she could do now.

I’ll live. I’ll survive, and then justice will be—

Justice?

Just what was justice?

Ly’s parents and siblings flashed through her mind.

To her all their faces looked like that of ferocious tigers with fangs.

.

–Ly’s body hit the river.

But it wasn’t as deep as she’d hoped it would be.

.

Her blood and the twin swords flowed along the river.

They were bound to reach the forest of Held eventually.
