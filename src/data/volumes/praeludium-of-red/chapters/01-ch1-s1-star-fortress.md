Chapter 1

Section 1 ——The Star Fortress——

♣ Yukina ~In the Former Lucifenia Territory, “Town of Retasan/Main Street”~

The fortified city of Retasan.

To me this town was just a transitional stop on my
journey; it wasn’t an area with any special meaning to it.

Once I’d crossed the border from here and headed east,
I’d arrive in the Empire of Beelzenia. My destination was there. I had arrived
here only three days ago, but I intended to get my departure permissions soon
and leave town.

I wasn’t in any real hurry. But having said that, the
town’s only real notable features were the fort walls that enclosed its
surroundings in the shape of a star. And I had almost no interest in military
installations.

I left the inn where I was staying, “The Half Moon Pavilion”,
and walked along the main street that led to the fort gate. The road had a large
stream of people walking on it, and it was overflowing with energy. When I
looked around I saw several different shops lined up; hardware stores, tailors,
fruit sellers, general goods stores, and spice merchants. I advanced quickly
along while holding my brown suitcase in both hands.

The suitcase was of sturdy make. Thanks to that it hadn’t
broken once during the entire one year trip, but as I was a fourteen year
old girl it was a little heavy for me to carry. Even so, that was something I’d
already grown accustomed to.

Right then, a slightly strong wind blew through.

“Ah!”

The hat with a ribbon tied around it that I was wearing
flew into the air. After passing by overhead of the pedestrians, it touched
down in front of a locksmith. When I dashed over to get it, a woman who was
passing by picked it up first.

She looked to be in her mid-twenties. The blonde haired
woman smiled and set my hat back down on my head.

Looking closely, the woman was wearing a soldier’s
uniform. She wore the national emblem of the old Lucifenian kingdom on her left
breast. I had been able to see a considerable number of soldiers among the
people walking along the road. It wasn’t that unusual–the town was right along
the country’s border, and it was pretty strongly considered to be a fortress.

“You have my thanks,” I nodded, expressing my
gratitude. Still smiling, she waved her hand and started heading in the
opposite direction to me.

I didn’t think it was a bad town. It was just that I
wanted to leave it as soon as I could–to speak more frankly, I wanted to leave
the country as soon as I could.

It had been over one year since I’d left Marlon against
my parents’ wishes.

When I’d first begun my trip, I had been a sheltered girl
who didn’t know anything. I was ignorant of the ways of the world, not even
knowing how to shop or reserve an inn.

I saw snow for the first time in Levianta to the north. I
was taught how to use a sword for self-defense by mercenaries in Beelzenia to
the east. Although, I had no knack for it at all, so I hadn’t picked up a sword
since then. Personally I thought that I was far more suited to wielding a pen
than a sword after all.

And then the place that I went next was here, in
Lucifenia.

Up until five years ago, Lucifenia had been one fully
fledged country called the “Kingdom of Lucifenia”. But due to the
revolution brought about by the people who were sick of the tyranny of the
princess at the time, Riliane Lucifen d'Autriche, the Lucifen dynasty was
destroyed.

Without a leader, Lucifenia ended up being under the
proxy rule of the country of Marlon in the west. That had been internationally
recognized as a temporary matter to the last, but four years ago the king of
Marlon, Kyle Marlon, had forced Lucifena to be annexed as Marlon’s territory.

There came criticism towards this from both inside and
outside the country, but the Marlon royal family ignored it, and furthermore ended
up waging war against the neighboring countries using Lucifenia as its starting
point.

And in that way, with barely a break, the state of
affairs once again descended into instability.

Frankly, I’d have to say that stepping foot in Lucifenia
in the middle of all that had been terrible.

The soldiers of this country didn’t look kindly on a
foreigner who wouldn’t reveal her social status, and I often had my movements
restricted. That wasn’t even the worst of it–One time I was even falsely
suspected of something and imprisoned.

At that point, in the end I’d been able to escape
hardship by using my last resort. To make a long explanation short, my father
was a wealthy merchant who was well known in all of Evillious, and he had a lot
of acquaintances among Lucifenia’s elite. Or to sum up, I used my connections.

In any case, fed up with all the complications, I decided
to quickly get out of the country; thus I ended up coming to this town that was
along the border with the eastern Beelzenian Empire.

Truthfully I would
have liked to stay here and see more of the places related to the “Three
Heroes” and “Daughter of Evil”.

It was regrettable but there was nothing to be done about
it. I’d resolved that I would come back to this country again someday after the
situation had calmed down.

After walking on a short while, finally I came to see a
large iron door before me, and an earth colored fortification wall that towered
over it.

Once I’d passed through this Retasan Fortress gate, I would
be able to head to the Empire of Beelzenia beyond it.

However, it was there that I ended up receiving an
unexpected setback.

“You can’t pass through here,” a young soldier
declared coldly. I had gotten quite used to such haughty behavior from the soldiers
of the Lucifenian Territory, so I asked the reason why I couldn’t leave without
any timidity.

“Military reasons.”

“That doesn’t give me much to go on. Please explain
it to me properly.”

When I continued to press the matter, the expression of
the soldier–who’d answered like it was an annoyance to do so–changed to one
of irritation.

“I don’t need to explain to civilians. In any case,
if you can’t pass through then you can’t
pass through.”

Taking
umbrage at his reply, I said snidely, “What an oppressive response. It’s
stuff like that that got you people occupied by Marlon.”

My words seemed to destroy the soldier’s good spirits. He
yelled loudly, rambling, “You insolent brat! I
can cut you down right here, right now! First off, what’s up with a traveler wearing
such gaudy clothes?! What’s an idiot like you doing here, journeying in a red
dress like that!? You don’t know anything about the world!”

He vented his rage at me. But as I couldn’t come up
with any good rebuttals on the topic of my clothing right then, I just glared
at the soldier as hard as I could.

A soldier somewhere else began to come over, as though unable
to let the confrontation slide. This one looked to be significantly older.

“We’re very sorry, miss. But we were given orders
that no one come through here for a little while.”

“That’s why I’m asking the reason.”

“We can’t just jabber on about military secrets with
you. You’re just a civilian, miss. I’m sorry, but please try to
understand.”

His manner was tender, but in the end the fact that I
couldn’t pass through was the same.

“You can’t, say, let me pass as special
circumstances?”

As I spoke, I drew closer to the soldier and gently held
his hand. When I moved away again, I’d left a gold coin in his palm. Basically,
a bribe. It wasn’t a very polite way of doing things, but you couldn’t make an
omelet without breaking a few eggs. I’d used this tactic at the checking
station while going from Levianta to Asmodean too.

But the elderly soldier kindly–yet firmly enough to make
his intentions clear–returned the gold.

“Sorry, but I can’t accept this. Retasan Fortress’s Commander
Mouchet is very strict about stuff like this. If I’m
caught, I’ll get killed for sure.”

“So there’s nothing I can do…?”

“Indeed, nothing. I don’t want to die just yet, you
see.”

“Are they really such a frightening person, this
Commander Mouchet?”

“They’re an ogre, I’m telling you. If you don’t want to be eaten up by the ogre,
you’d better hurry back.”

I could keep persevering on it, but it looked like I couldn’t
hope for a better outcome. I gave up and decided to head back to the inn for
now.

The “Half Moon Pavilion” was a two story inn
with seven rooms in all; right now, there were guests staying in five of the
rooms.

After I’d somewhat awkwardly explained the situation, having
already checked out once before, the petite, middle-aged owner let me check in
again, grinning.

“Ain’t got no reason to refuse. Filling up a room
deserves a warm welcome.”

All of the guest rooms were upstairs–the downstairs was
for the owner’s room, and the bar. The bar also functioned as a dining
hall, so the residents would take their meals there.

I was hungry, so I decided to have some lunch. For now I
would think about what I was going to do next while I was eating. The food was charged
separately, so I took out two coins from my pocket and handed it to the skinny employee.
After replying cheerfully to me, he called to the kitchen,

“Get me one white Rollam Bird paella!”

That was the most delicious out of the dishes they served
there. That was the conclusion I’d come to. Their omelets weren’t bad either,
but they were very salty, so they were a little much to eat every day.

The bar was quiet. It was still midday, so while I knew
there were hardly any guests drinking, it wouldn’t have been out of place for
there to be others looking to catch a meal like me. But there were only about
five people there, including me.

It was always like this when I ate, so I imagined it
wasn’t a very popular dining hall. It would get fairly crowded at night, so
perhaps the locals thought of it more strongly as a bar.

Hm? That woman…

I saw a face I recognized amongst the guests. It was the
female soldier. The one who’d picked up my hat for me just a short while ago.

“Hey, uh–Thank you very much for earlier,” I said
boldly. I thought that taking my meal with someone else might be more fun, and I
also had the ulterior motive that,
since she was a soldier, I might be able to get her to tell me the reason for
why I couldn’t pass through the gate.

“Ah, you’re the…Well, no thanks needed.”

“Do you mind if I join you?”

She was sitting alone at a table for four. When I pointed
to the chair across from her, she nodded agreeably.

“Go ahead. How ‘bout we eat together while you’re
here, huh?”

She talked a little bit like a man, perhaps because she
was a member of the military. I thought it a bit unsuited to how pretty her
face looked, at the same time. If she were to attend a dinner party wearing a
dress, she would probably be popular with the men there.

Once I sat down she said, “You a traveler?”

“Yes, how could you tell?”

“Never seen your face around here, and you’ve got
that huge honking suitcase. Whaddya think of the town?”

“I think it’s very nice. Although the walls
encompassing its surroundings feel a little constraining.”

“Yeah, it’s sorta like a prison isn’t it?”

After a short while of rambling conversation, finally the
employee brought out our food.

“Your white Rollam bird paella, thank you for
waiting!”

It seemed she’d ordered the same thing as me, as there
were two dishes of paella lined up on the table.

“This is the stuff,” she said, smiling and
bringing the food to her mouth.

“I agree. Bon appétit.” I returned a laugh and
began eating, myself.

After we went through the meal for a short while, she asked
me frankly, “Hey, weren’t you heading to the fortress gate before? How
come you’re back here?”

“Well, I’d been wanting to head out of the country
to go to the Empire of Beelzenia, but I don’t have permission to leave. …I’ve
had to just come back here for now.”

“Oh, so that’s it, huh? …What’s yer name?”

“My name is Yukina.”

During my travels I had taken to only telling people my
first name when asked. Considering the influence that the “Freezis”
name held, I wanted to avoid declaring it as often as I could.

She didn’t seem to pay much mind to the fact that I had
withheld my last name.

“Yukina, it sucks but you might have a hard time
getting to Beelzenia. Things are pretty tense between them and Lucifenia right
now.”

“Things are tense?”

“Yeah. The country of Lucifenia originally got so
big out of stealing territory from the Empire of Beelzenia, after all. The two
countries have never been on great terms.”

“But Lucifenia’s not a kingdom anymore.”

“Doesn’t matter. The head might be different, but
the country’s body is still the same. On a fundamental level.” She set the
spoon she was holding in her right hand down on the table. “Even after
becoming governed by Marlon, Lucifenia and Beelzenia still had their skirmishes
for one thing or another. On that point, I recently got some information from
the higher ups in the military; that Beelzenia’s harboring a fearsome criminal that’s
done harm to the Marlon royal family.”

“A criminal, you say? Who in the world is
that?” I asked, leaning in.

“Ah–as you might expect, that’s confidential.
Sorry.” Perhaps realizing that she’d said too much, she raised her hands
to her chest and gestured as though to rein me in. “Anyway, there’s a
pretty high chance of some kind of fight breaking out between the two countries
from all this. So we’re to restrict all movement in and out of the country.”

“You mean the territory of Lucifenia might invade Beelzenia?”

"If they don’t hand over the criminal to us,
yeah.“

A war could start over just one criminal. What a
grandiose idea. I’d become intrigued as to just what kind of criminal this was,
but going off of what happened just now there wasn’t any chance she’d tell.

Even so, she had spoken more concretely on it with me
than the soldiers at the gate. The important point was that I couldn’t go to
Beelzenia due to some arbitrary circumstances with both countries. Irritated, I
hit the table. The silverware rattled slightly as I knocked on the wood.

"Good grief, can’t these people stop recklessly
starting wars for their own interests?! It’s obvious this criminal is just an
excuse to expand their territory!”

I had raised my voice without thinking, but I quickly
realized that was a blunder for two reasons. The first being that it was
uncouth for a lady to shout during her meal, and the second being that I had just
criticized the country right in front of someone who was related to the
military.

But she didn’t look displeased or angry–rather, she
laughed loudly. “Ha ha ha! Exactly. Marlon’s current policy of military
expansion doesn’t sit well with the public. And I agree.”

That was a little surprising. Normally someone in the
military wouldn’t voice such overt criticism of the government. Depending on
the circumstance they could be charged with treason and imprisoned.

Maybe because she’s
a woman she isn’t very high ranking?

The yellow uniform that she was wearing was of the old
Lucifenian military. Most of the soldiers for the current Lucifenian territory
wore the blue uniforms of Marlon. Maybe she was being made to wear an old
uniform due to not being very distinguished.

Having received such unexpected endorsement, against my
better judgment I spoke more excitedly.

“I’m glad you understand! I was treated so coldly by
some soldiers at the gate a little while ago. The top brass are so unreliable,
they probably don’t even bother to train their troops properly!”

In truth it was just the younger soldier at the gate’s
behavior that I’d had a problem with, but from the flow of conversation I had
moved into criticisms of the military higher ups.

“Heh heh heh, I guess so.”

“After all, isn’t this fortress’ Commander Mouchet a
good for nothing bum too? You…I’m sorry, I haven’t asked your name.”

“Name? Uh, Lily.”

“Lily. That’s a very nice name. Naturally, you know
about this Commander Mouchet person, don’t you Miss Lily?”

“Haha, well, yeah.”

Lily seemed to be holding back a smile. Judging by her
behavior, this Commander Mouchet wasn’t very popular after all.

“The guards at the gate told me that Commander
Mouchet is frightening like an ogre, cruel like a demon, and works the soldiers
like slaves. It must be such a burden on you to have to work under such a
person as that.”

“Oh, they said that, did they?”

“Here’s what I think. The current Lucifenian
Territory’s army ought to be headed by someone like you, who understands how
things work–”

Right then, the door to the bar opened and in came a tall
soldier.

“This is where you’ve been?” The soldier walked
up to Lily and saluted her. “General Ausdin is here from his country. He’s
currently waiting in your room.”

“Got it. I’ll go in a minute, once I’m done eating.
By the by, who were the soldiers charged with guarding the gate today?”

“Ah. I think it was Bonnard and Oudinot.”

“Bonnard and Oudinot, huh? Right. I’ll keep that in
mind, heheh…”

“Well then, please come at your earliest
convenience, Commander Mouchet.”

The tall soldier bowed once, and then left the bar.

“Um—uh, Ms. Lily?” I asked timidly, “Is it alright if I
ask what your—your full name is?”

“Mhm, yep. Riliane Mouchet. But I don’t care for being
called ‘Riliane’ so I usually go by Lily.”

“Um, I…A thousand apologies.”

“Come on, eat up. Before the food gets cold.”

The paella there was delicious. But I can’t remember what
it tasted like that day.

Five days passed.

“You looking for shoes, Miss Yukina?” I heard
Lily call to me as I was browsing before a shoe store.

“You seem in good spirits, Commander Mouchet.”

“Just Lily is fine. It’s a bit weird to get addressed
that way by anyone other than my subordinates.”

After that day, Lily had often met with me in town again.
Apparently reclining in the commander’s room didn’t suit her, so outside of emergencies
it seemed she often would come to see how things were in town like she was now.

During those five days there were several things I learned
about Lily.

First, she was adored by the people in town. It had only
been two years since she had assumed office as commander of the Retasan
Fortress, but she had adapted to the town like she’d been born and raised there
herself.

And it seemed that popularity was the same among her
underlings. They were intimidated by her, harsh and courageous as she was, but on
the other hand she was trusted by them, and well-liked.

And then, there was her name. Lily’s real name was
“Riliane”, but as she apparently disliked being addressed by that name, if any
of her underlings called her that by accident her expression would suddenly turn
displeased. It was only natural that she would feel that way. The name Riliane
was the same as the name of the princess who was the cause of the old
Lucifenian Kingdom’s destruction, the “Daughter of Evil”.

The names’ origins were both the same, each taken from
the princess’ great grandmother, Empress Riliane Roses.

Lily’s father, the famous general Gaston Mouchet, received
permission to give that name to his daughter because she was a distant relative
of the Mouchet family, and in appreciation of his service towards the Kingdom
of Lucifenia. Gaston was likely ecstatic, thinking it a great honor.  There’s no way he could have guessed that his daughter would end
up shunning that name twenty five years later.

General Gaston Mouchet had also once been the commander
of the Retasan Fortress, but he was defeated in a one-on-one fight with the
“Masked Man” during the revolution, and died. Upholding her father’s
teachings that one ought not to hold a grudge for life and death during a war,
Lily apparently didn’t have any desire for revenge against the revolution and
the “Masked Man”.

“In the end, my success is also riding on my
father’s coat-tails.”

She had said that about herself to be modest, but it
seemed she hated more than anything to be teased about that by other people. I
was certain that she worked harder than anyone so that no one would say that
about her. Her popularity with her underlings and townspeople was proof of
that.

There was another reason for how fond people were of her.
That was her kindness; that if there was anyone in need of help, even if she
didn’t know them, she would aid them without exception.

Actually, Lily had promised that she would arrange things
so that I might go to Beelzenia somehow.

“Well, when it comes to getting out of the country I
think things’ll work out. It’s getting into Beelzenia that’ll be tricky,”
she said, sighing quietly. There was no border police that would readily accept
a visitor from an enemy country in the midst of such tense circumstances. She
asked, a strange look on her face, “Why do you want to get there in the
first place?”

“There’s an old castle that I want to go see.”

“An old castle, huh? You an old relic enthusiast, Miss
Yukina?”

“Oh no, that’s not really it…”

“Well, I suppose trips are often taken for stuff
like that.”

It seemed Lily had come to a conclusion on her own
without waiting for me to speak.

At any rate, getting to Beelzenia with the way things
were now would be difficult. It seemed that I had no choice but to use that method to make things go more
smoothly.

I guess I’ll have
to rely on the influence of the “Freezis family”.

The name of my father, Keel Freezis, was known throughout
Evillious. I felt no sense of debt towards taking advantage of my parentage,
but using the Freezis name had good consequences and bad ones. One of the
things I’d learned from my travel experiences was that it was safer not to indiscreetly
make a display of myself unless it was necessary.

That was especially true here in Lucifenia. My father was
friends with the Kyle, the king of Marlon that was currently governing
Lucifenia. If King Kyle were to by some chance learn of my whereabouts, there
was a big possibility that he’d tell my father. I wanted to avoid that if I
could.

“Miss Lily. Is it alright if I ask you a
favor?” I started, broaching the discussion.

“What is it? Want me to buy you some shoes?”

“I do need new shoes. The ones I’m wearing now are
extremely worn out. But I’ll pay for that myself. I have something else I need
you to do.”

“Yeah? Well then, shoot.”

“I need you to send a messenger to Beelzenia.”

“I can do that, but what for? I can’t send out a
messenger on a useless task. There’s a chance their life would be in
danger.”

I told Lily the particulars. She looked a little
surprised, but cheerfully agreed.

The result of that came about eighteen days later.

That morning I wasn’t woken by the shrill cry of a white Rollam
bird, nor the alarm bell of the fortress soldiers on watch, but by Lily calling
to me from outside the Half Moon Pavilion.

She came into the inn before I could reply; I heard the
sound of what must be her noisily stomping up the stairs, and then she vigorously
threw open the door.

“What’s wrong? You’re here so early.”

“You up, Yukina? Let’s go. We’ve preparations to
make.”

Being spoken to so brusquely by her, while she wore a
military uniform, made me feel like I was some prisoner or criminal being taken
somewhere. Or maybe that was just because of the walls that encompassed the
town in a star pattern. Lily had said earlier that it was like a prison.

Anyhow, I certainly couldn’t ignore an order from Lily,
the commander of the whole Retasan Fortress army. Despite not knowing the reason
why, I quickly got out of bed, hurriedly got dressed, and did up my hair.

“Where’s your suitcase? I’ll help carry it,”
Lily asked, either out of simple kindness or irritation with how sluggish I was
upon just waking up.

“Huh? Am I going to be using my suitcase, Miss
Lily?”

“Of course. You’re leaving the country today.”

At last I finally understood what it was she was getting
at.

It had been twenty six days since I’d arrived at this
town that was close to the border.

I had finally been given permission to enter the Empire
of Beelzenia.

“Once they got your name it seems they immediately
gave you the OK. The Freezis family’s reputation is pretty impressive. Or else
you’ve got a friend over there… Anyway, thank goodness for that, huh?”

Lily explained to me the circumstances as we were walking
on the road to the fortress gate, after I’d checked out of the inn.

She had always been a fast walker, but it seemed she was
especially quick today. I desperately struggled to keep up with her while
carrying along my suitcase.

It wasn’t very far from the inn to the fortress gate.
After we were clear of the road of stalls, bustling since that morning, that
big iron door came into my line of vision.

A soldier standing upright next to the door immediately
saluted upon noticing Lily, and the other one followed suit.

“The girl’s going through. Open the gate.”

“Yes sir!”

One of the soldiers turned an iron crank to the left of
the gate, and it gradually began to open. After that I would be able to see the
road to Beelzenia beyond it. Or so I thought–unfortunately it seemed my
expectations hadn’t been accurate. Once the door had opened all the way, it was
still blocked off by a wooden slab.

This time the other soldier began to turn a crank on the
right side, and the wooden slab began to incline further in time with his
motions. When I looked more closely I was able to determine that there were
chains connected to the upper side of the slab, and that there was a very deep
moat on the other side of the door and walls.

“Hurry up!”

“Yes sir!”

Lily pressed the soldiers to go faster. I felt that was a
little unusual. What was she so impatient over?

“Miss Lily, is it alright if I ask you
something?”

“…? What is it?”

“Are you trying to run me out of the country?”

“Huh!? …Ah, sorry. That’s not why I’m doing all
this. The upper brass have done something a little bothersome, that’s
all.”

“Something bothersome?”

“It seems the king himself has personally dispatched
some of his troops this way. They’re due to arrive this afternoon. Well, anyway
I thought it would be safer to get you sent off to Beelzenia while I can still do as I like. You don’t want
the country to find out your parentage, right?”

“Ah, so you’re doing this for my sake. Thank you
very much.”

I thanked her honestly, but I also wished that she had
explained that to me while we were walking. Seemed she had a tendency to prefer
action over brief explanations.

When the slab had become level with the ground it bridged
the gap between here and the other side of the moat, and I was able to go on
ahead. What I had thought was a mere slab had actually been a drawbridge.

“If you go along this road about half a day you
should be able to see Rucolebeni, territory of the Beelzenian Empire.

Word should have reached the checkpoints on the way
there, so

if you hand over this pass then you’ll be able to get through.”

So saying, she handed me a sheet of paper.

Rucolebeni! A place
related to the vampiress, Vanika!

I hadn’t been this excited since visiting the ruins of
the Venomania mansion in Asmodean. This time for sure I might be able to
approach the heart of the matter regarding these legends of the “Vessels
of Deadly Sin” that I’ve only been able to read about in books.

The “Vessels of Deadly Sin” were devilish tools
said to house “Seven Demons”. I didn’t know if such artifacts really
existed or not. But visiting places that were related to them was one of the
reasons for my current journey.

“Thank you for all of your help, Miss Lily.”

"You take care. War might break out between here and
Beelzenia. If you get caught up in it I want you to get out immediately. Got
it?“

"Yeah. I’ll bear that in mind.”

“You’re very level-headed, but even so you’re still
just a fourteen year old girl. Stick to what you’re able to do. Make sure you
don’t do anything rash. If you die I’ll have nightmares about it, and your
fans’ll be upset too.”

“Fans?”

“You’re a writer aren’t you? One of my subordinates
knows the name Yukina Freezis.”

It wasn’t that I’d been trying to hide the fact that I
was a writer. I just hadn’t said everything there was to say about me. I was
still nameless as an author, and I as yet was still severely lacking in merit.

Yes, the writings of a sheltered girl who knew nothing of
the world could never truly shake people’s hearts.

It was for that reason that I went on my journey.

To learn more of this world, and learn the truth.
