Chapter 2

Section 1 ——Hometown of Misgivings——

♣ Yukina ~The Country of Marlon, “Capital Bariti”~

It wasn’t raining, but the sky was
pretty cloudy. The black-roofed carriage that we rode in advanced along the
thin, crudely cobbled road.

The carriage frequently swayed,
probably because Marlon highways were so much more poorly maintained than those
on the mainland. I was alright for the most part, but there was someone I was
worried about.

Is
Miss Germaine alright?

I stuck my head out the window and
peered into the carriage next to mine. As I thought, in there right then was a
swordswoman whose face was pale as death, and a composed sorceress who didn’t
look the least bit concerned.

I hadn’t paid any mind to how poorly
laid out the stonework on the highways was before going out on my journey. Even
that much showed to me how much discernment I’d gained in that year-and-a-half.

“How’s Bariti? It’s been a
while for you,” my brother asked, sitting in the seat next to me.

How was it? I had no answer for him.
Bariti’s townscape, the drab red brick shops, the broken state of the black bar
signs, and the enormous clocktower of the great church that I could see looming
in the northern sky, had not changed at all from one year ago.

Despite having asked the question,
Shaw himself didn’t seem terribly interested in my answer. His gaze looked to
be directed past me and more at Gumillia, who he could see through the window
in the neighboring carriage. In my head I muttered, That precocious brat! , but out loud I merely replied “It’s
pretty nostalgic~”

Three carriages were on their way to
my home, the Freezis estate. Bariti was divided into north and a south by the Methis
river that flowed through the center, and the Freezis estate was in the north.

In the carriage I was riding in,
aside from Shaw there were two of the attendant men in black sitting in the
seat behind me. In the carriage moving along to our left there was Germaine and
Gumillia, as well as two men in black uniforms like with us. In the remaining
carriage there were only men in black.

One other carriage had traveled with
us until we’d arrived in Bariti. It was the carriage King Kyle was riding in.
That carriage had separated from us halfway through, and headed for the Marlon
castle in the west. The navymen, on the other hand, were probably in the middle
of a party at the port right about now.

We gradually approached the
clocktower of the great church that I had seen from a distance. As this was a
church of the Levia sect I wasn’t terribly
familiar with it, me and my whole family being members of the Held sect and
all.

It hadn’t always been like that, but
the Levia sect was becoming mainstream even in Marlon. There were no longer any
churches to Held in the country, so the Freezis family had no custom of going
to a church to pray.

Only my mother, who was the most
devout member of my entire family, would sometimes walk to far off towns or
even other countries to attend a Held church.

What…what
is this?

A line had formed before the church. It was composed
of old and young, men and women–various people.

“They’re getting
medicine,” Shaw began to explain, before I could ask. He’d promptly
noticed that I was interested. As one might expect of a pair of siblings.
“They’re sufferers of the Gula disease. Seems the church is dispensing
medicine for free.”

So the Gula disease had spread even
to Marlon. Apparently it was only in the last year or so, so it had begun to
take hold after I had already set out on my journey.

The Gula disease–I remembered what
happened in the Beelzenian Empire, and shuddered.

“Are there a lot of people
dying from it?” I timidly asked Shaw.

“In Bariti, medicine is pretty
well circulated. And the church manages poor people like they’re doing over
there. I’ve hardly heard any talk of anyone dying from the disease.
Except…”

“Except what?”

“I’ve heard there are some
places out in the countryside where things have gotten to be pretty terrible. Especially
in the area around Castle Hedgehog to the northwest.

I had no wish to encounter those
dead soldiers again, but as I couldn’t deny the possibility that the owner of
the Glass of Conchita–Ney–was in Marlon, I had to be prepared for that.

The people in the line were thin,
and had lost their vitality. I prayed for their full recovery from inside the
carriage as it passed by the church.

♣ Yukina ~The Country of Marlon, “Freezis Estate/Foyer”~

“The house is as big as
ever.”

That was the impression I was
brought to standing in front of my home for the first time in over a year. Once
Shaw heard that, he tilted his head to the side.

“What? You’ve only just noticed?”

To my little brother, the fact that
the house was “big” was obvious. But for me it was different.

The first time I’d stayed in an inn
after setting out, I had thought it was so small.
That was my sense of values at the time. But now I realized that that inn
hadn’t been “small”, it was this house that was “big”.

The house’s exterior hadn’t changed
at all since I’d left. I knew that what had changed wasn’t the house, but me.

Descending from the carriage in a
line, I headed for the area before the foyer ahead of Shaw. There, a single boy
wearing a servant’s uniform was on standby.

“Welcome home, Master
Shaw.”

I didn’t know this servant. Was he
hired after I went out on my trip?

“I’ve brought my sister and
some guests. Could you tell Dad?”

The servant boy made his reply and
then disappeared into the house to call on my father.

Standing behind me, Germaine
tactfully suggested, “You’re going to be reuniting with your parents again,
right? Should we step away for a minute?”

I wanted to accept her proposal, but
it was probably better that she and Gumillia stayed put. It would make
everything a bit less of a hassle.

“Don’t worry about it,” was all I replied.

Immediately upon entering the foyer,
it opened up into a long hallway. My father’s room was all the way down it, to
the left.

After a short period, I could see a
figure dashing towards us from that direction with all his might. Even though I
couldn’t see him clearly it was obvious to me who it was.

In truth, until I’d arrived here
now, I hadn’t been able to decide in what manner I should accept my
confrontation with my father. I had even calculated that if I innocently
embraced him and said “Papa~!” or something like that, like when I
was a child, I might be able to mitigate his scolding somewhat. Shaw had said
as much before we departed from Lucifenia, but considering Dad’s personality
there was very little chance he’d be furious with me. Even so, to make doubly
sure of something was the way of a clever girl like me.

But in the end, I decided to see
what he would do first, without doing anything extravagant myself.

“Y~u~ki~na~! Welcome home~!”

My father headed for me at full
speed. And then he opened his arms wide, and hugged me as hard as he could. All
as I was expecting, including the fact that his cheeks were wet with tears.

After this I’d probably be made to
hear him go on and on about how worried he was about me, and how lonely he’d been.
Thinking about what I’d done perhaps that was my just desserts, but as that
would probably be a little annoying, it was an event I would have liked to refuse if
possible.

“It’s good to see you too, Papa.
This is Miss Germaine and Miss Gumillia.”

I made my response to his welcome
and then quickly introduced him to the two others with me. This obviously
wasn’t their first meeting. But I did so anyway.

“Hm? Ah, long time no see, you
two.” Dad hurriedly wiped his tear-soaked glasses and straightened up. “Since
you’re here, try to make yourself comfortable. I’ve got a few things I’d like
to ask you.”

In a moment he returned back to the countenance
of Keel Freezis, head of the merchant trade association. I was right to bring
Germaine and Gumillia along after all.

I knew that while my father was an
amazingly doting parent, more than that he was also someone who put priority on
his appearance as a merchant. I was able to delay some annoyance with that for
now. Although well–I probably ought to talk to my father again when things had
calmed down again and I was able to recharge from my trip.

“You come along too, Yukina. We’re
making preparations for supper.”

My father spoke while maintaining a
level of gravity, unlike how he’d been earlier.

“Alright, but first I want to
go see Aile and Mother.”

“I see, then while you’re at it
please call the two of them to the dining hall. We’re going to have dinner with
the whole family together this time.”

My father started to turn around,
but he stopped, seeming to have realized something, and then looked around the foyer.

“Come to think of it, where’s
Kyle? Did he not come with you?”

“I advised him to return to
Marlon castle first. I thought that important he do that as king.”

“Well…That being said…”

Appearing accepting despite looking a
little dissatisfied, my father headed for the dining hall, taking Gumillia and
Germaine with him.

One of the men in black spoke to
Shaw. “Well then, we shall take our leave.”

“Thank you for your time. Get
some rest today.”

With the men in black gone, that
just left me and Shaw.

“You should go to Mom’s room,
sis. I’m gonna go see Aile while you’re doing that.”

“Alright. By the way,
Shaw.”

“Hm, what is it?”

I didn’t myself know why I was
saying something like this. But I spontaneously blurted out, “Has
Papa…changed from before?”

Shaw looked back at me with a
somewhat bemused expression. “I think the one who’s
changed isn’t Papa, but you, sis.”

“Yeah…I guess so. Sorry for
asking something so weird.”

Shaw shrugged, and started to head
for Aile’s room.

♣ Yukina ~The Country of Marlon, “Freezis Estate/Mikina’s Room”~

As I got closer to my mother’s room,
I came to hear the sound of pleasant harp music. She was probably playing it.

When I opened the door, the music
stopped. Inside was my mother, who–like my father–hadn’t changed at all from
how I remembered her.

Once she saw it was me, her face
suddenly brightened.

“…Welcome home, Yukina.”

“Thank you, Mama.”

We hugged each other, and rejoiced
at our long-awaited reunion.

“I think you’ve gotten a little
taller.”

“Hm–I guess.”

“Did you enjoy your trip? Did
you get any stomach aches from eating unfamiliar food?”

“No, it was fine. Hey,
Mama.”

“What?”

“It’s been so long, I’d like to
hear you play the harp.”

“Haha, alright. We’ll be having
supper soon, so I’ll only play a little.”

I took a seat, Mom too sat at her
harp, and she resumed her music once more.

My mother was a basically reserved
woman, and modest despite continuing to support my father behind the scenes. I
had heard from her own mouth that though she was originally the daughter of a prestigious
family she had fallen so head over heels for my father that as a result of her
passion she’d run away with him to Elphegort when they eloped.

Her sole hobby was playing the harp.
Dad had encouraged her to play at his dinner banquets, but she’d always
refused, claiming to be too shy. The only people who were able to hear her
music were the members of my family.

Before, I would often listen to my
mother’s harp while indulging in my reading. Hearing such familiar music, I
finally felt the sense of having returned home well up inside me.

“Mama…I’m sorry.”

“For what?” she replied,
continuing to play the harp.

“For going out on my own and
making you worry.”

While I was saying that I remembered
that I needed to apologize to my father properly too.

Mom didn’t say anything for a short
period, merely smiling and keeping up the music.

Eventually, she reached a good
pausing point and stopped playing. The moment that she opened her mouth to say
something, someone opened the door and came into the room.

“Big sis, you’re home!”

A girl who looked a little older
than I remembered was there.

“Thank you, Aile.”

Aile dashed to me, and innocently
hugged me. She then started coughing violently, probably from being
overexcited.

“Now now, all this running…”

My mother pat Aile’s back with a
concerned expression.

Aile was my younger sister, under
four years old. Perhaps because she had more strongly inherited my mother’s
disposition, unlike me and Shaw she was a very meek type. She’d been a little
sickly since she was a baby, and there were times when my mother had to nurse
her back to health.

Mom took care of Aile herself, and
wasn’t in the habit of entrusting her to the servants.

I didn’t know why
that was, but if I were to put together all the rumors the servants exchanged,
it had something to do with the incident that their caretaker had caused when
Shaw was a baby.

I didn’t know the specific details
on that, but I had always thought it was connected to the faint burn scars left
on Shaw’s back, and my mother seldom hiring Elphe people as servants out of her
unusual hatred for them.

Even mother’s reserved nature was
for Aile’s sake too. I wasn’t around her much as a result, and there were times
when that made me pretty lonely.

At times like that, my father would
always treat me warmly. Even when he was busy, he would always take any spare
moment he could to care for me. I was glad for that, and had always depended on
him.

Since when had I started to feel adverse
to my father’s affection?

…Yeah,
I really should apologize to him later.

The door opened again, and Shaw
peered inside.

“Everyone should head to the
dining hall soon. I’m starving.”

Mom stood and clapped her hands.

“Yes yes, how about we all go
together? It’s been so long since we’ve all eaten as a family–haha, Mama’s
looking forward to it.”

♣ Yukina ~The Country of Marlon, “Freezis Estate/Dining Hall”~

The seven person banquet between the
entire Freezis Family (including myself), Germaine, and Gumillia, was held in a
gentle atmosphere from start to finish.

I hadn’t eaten in my home in a long
time. Luxurious meals managed by veteran cooks from our time living in
Elphegort were lined up on the table.

Thick slices of roast beef, stiff
baked potatoes–and the cooking of the vegetable garnished pudding was exquisite
too.

It seemed our guests were also quite
satisfied with the food. Germaine in particular was chowing down so heartily
that it was hard to believe she’d been nauseous earlier.

“It’s the first time I’ve ever
eaten it, but this Marlon cooking is just delicious.”

As Germaine expressed her opinion,
my father made a sarcastic laugh, shoulders shaking a little and looking
somewhat proud.

“I’m glad for the praise, but I
think the dinners in our home are a little bit different from proper Marlon
cuisine. If pushed, I’d have to say with the seasoning and such that it’s
closer to Elphegort cooking.”

“Oh yeah?”

“Indeed. If you’d like to sample
real Marlon food, you should try out a nearby bar when you get the chance…Though
I wouldn’t recommend it.”

Dad quietly laughed again.

Generally speaking there was a lot
of Marlon traditional cooking that was light on the flavor. My parents, both
being raised in Marlon, were able to accept that–but I and my brother had
lived in Elphegort since we were young, and so it was a little wearisome …to be
plain it was extremely unappetizing. I simply couldn’t eat out while I was in
Marlon.

After Germaine drank up her sherry,
my father advised, “It’s getting late tonight, and Kyle isn’t here. You’re
probably tired from your trip. We have some guest rooms prepared, so feel free
to rest here. We’ll have a more in depth conversation about things tomorrow.”

“That would be greatly
appreciated.” Germaine set her knife and fork on the table. “Today we
were attacked by pirates, and then chased by a giant octopus, terrible stuff.”

This time Mom and Dad set their
knife and fork down. Or rather, they didn’t set them down so much as dropped
them.

“Pirates…and a giant
octopus!?”

Germaine continued, oblivious to my
parents’ unrest. “It was pretty difficult~ Any more of that and we’d be
dead at sea…”

“Yukina…and Shaw…were put in
such danger…”

Dad started to tear up again. Mom’s
hand was trembling.

I could tell this wasn’t going anywhere
good. I quickly cut off the conversation.

“W-well, how about we leave the
rest of the story for tomorrow!”

“Y-yeah! Didn’t you say so
yourself, Dad? Everyone’s tired!”

With Shaw’s backing, we somehow
managed to calm things down.

If I told my parents the events of
my journey in full, they were liable to faint.

I would spend a bit
of time spacing out the tales of my travels.

♣ Yukina ~The Country of Marlon, “Freezis Estate/Yukina’s Room”~

This room, long bereft of its owner,
had been kept exactly as it was without so much as a scrap of dust, thanks to
our excellent servants.

A guest visiting for the first time
might have mistaken it for a boy’s room, or else a study. It was just an empty
space with countless books lined up on bookshelves on both sides of the room, no
decoration to be seen.

When I took in a deep breath there
was the faint, sweet scent of paper. I relaxed. This was none other than my
room, on the Freezis mansion’s second floor.

I slid onto the bed encamped in the
center and closed my eyes.

Germaine had said as much, but quite
a lot of stuff happened today. Battle with pirates and a giant octopus, a
reunion with my family…

Not just today, but this entire year
and a half journey had involved quite a lot of meetings.

I knew that I had changed on my
trip. Perhaps I had become a little bit stronger.

But it wasn’t enough yet. There was
still so much that I wanted to learn. I still hadn’t solved the mystery of the
“Vessels of Deadly Sin”. Someday, when I had become an adult, I would
go on another journey. In order to obtain that which I could not on this one.

I fell asleep thinking about such
things. As I was first dozing off I noticed a rustling sound coming from
outside my window.

There was a window to the east and
to the north in my room–the noise was coming from the north side. The Column Forest
was outside, but on a dark night like this I couldn’t see much out there
clearly.

I was able to catch sight of a
figure walking out to that forest. I didn’t see their face, but I could make
out short cut green hair and a sorcerer’s robe.

It’s
Miss Gumillia. What is she doing out this time of night?

Finally the figure disappeared into
the forest. I was concerned, but it was dangerous to enter the forest at night.
I couldn’t just chase after her.

The forest being dangerous at night
was the same for Gumillia as well, but I got the feeling that I didn’t need to
worry too much on her account. I had thought so back in the Millenium Tree
Forest as well, but she seemed like the kind of person who was used to forests,
even if it wasn’t in her homeland.

I once more got into bed, and tried
to reason out why Gumillia had gone into the woods.

There were certain times and places
that were suited to using magic. A forest at night was certainly the most ideal
for performing magical rituals. Gumillia herself had told me that.

From that I could glean that
Gumillia was probably either performing some spell of some kind, or else making
preparations for one. For what purpose? The answer was simple. To make provisions
against our “enemies”.

Yes,
I guess I’ve been thinking about this all wrong.

I’d been so at ease upon coming home
again that I’d completely forgotten.

Go out on a trip again? What was I
thinking? My current journey wasn’t over yet.

The shadow of conspiracy that
covered Marlon. The true identity of the one who had King Kyle possessed by a
demon, and Ney Phatipe. It wouldn’t be over until all of those things had been
settled.

♣ Yukina ~The Country of Marlon, “Freezis Estate/Keel’s Room”~

“I’ve heard various rumors
regarding you,” my father said, leisurely resting his elbows on the desk
in front of him.

Germaine was seated with her legs
crossed in a slightly impolite manner, facing him.

“I’ve just been
traveling.”

He widened his eyes a little at her blunt
response. “That so? I heard you raised a racket in Asmodean and
Beelzenia.”

“Goodness, seems you’re very
well informed of my actions. Nothing less than I’d expect from Keel
Freezis.”

When morning came, I’d been summoned
to Dad’s room. He and Germaine were already inside.

Gumillia had returned once, but then
after breakfast she had left again. My father had ordered the servants to try
and detain her, but it seemed she’d successfully snuck out.

There was no sign that King Kyle was
coming, so for the time being I guess Dad had decided to hear my and Germaine’s
side of the story.

“Although, that’s kind of
strange then.”

Germaine cocked her head to the side.
It appeared she had some doubts regarding my father knowing her deeds.

“What is?”

“It seemed to me that Kyle went
a very long time without knowing my whereabouts.”

He rested his chin in his hands, as
though to say “ah…so that’s it”.

“I didn’t bother to pass along
any of my information to him.”

Why was that? The two of them were
best friends. Why would he keep silent on what his best friend wanted to learn
more than anything?

Before I could voice my question,
Dad continued speaking. “There were several things that didn’t make sense
to me during the revolution five years ago, you see.” Germaine was silent.
He leaned forward slightly, and gazed at her. “As one of the interested
parties, you probably already know what those are, don’t you?”

Was he talking about the issue with
Michaela? In that case, I had obtained an answer during my travels. If he
didn’t know the truth, then I would have to tell it to him.

But Germaine turned her face away and
replied, “…I know, but I can’t tell you.”

When I had asked her about the
incident with Michaela, Germaine had clearly refused. Why was she obscuring her
answer now?

Or–was there a different mystery remaining
regarding the revolution?

The two of them continued speaking.

“I surmised you might say that.
So I kept quiet to Kyle, and did a little investigating on my own. My inquiry
into you was one part of that.”

“So then what did you learn
from your investigation?”

“Various things, but to bring
up the biggest one, it’s that the Empress Dowager Prim and the sorceress Abyss
I.R. appear to be involved with Kyle having been manipulated by some sort of
power.”

Abyss I.R. Gumillia had mentioned
that name too.

“Who is that?” I had been
silent, and at that point I first opened my mouth.

“She’s the Empress Dowager’s
resident sorceress. Though she doesn’t show herself often publicly.”

According to my father’s
explanation, Abyss I.R.’s origins were unknown, but apparently she had been
living in Marlon for quite some time. Her name had been seen close to twenty
years ago, so they said.

Germaine also listened in with great
interest.

“Miss Germaine, have you heard
the name Abyss I.R. before?”

“No, this is first time. What
kind of scheme is this sorceress dreaming up?”

My father sighed that he didn’t know
much himself. “To get involved with her, I would have to more deeply
investigate the royal family.”

“I imagine there’s quite a bit
of risk in that.”

“So around that point, my
investigation had hit a dead end. I had thought that I was fine with not
rushing to learn more, but…” My father once again widened his eyes,
looking at Germaine and then me. “Then there was the aforementioned
turmoil with you two.”

I could tell from the wrinkle of his
brow that he was just a little displeased.

Germaine replied with a light tone,
whether knowing of my father’s mental state or not, “So you’re saying you’re
taking it more seriously now that your daughter’s been wrapped up in all
this.”

“I am not an ally of
justice.” There was a rapping on the desk. The source of the noise was my
father’s right hand. “The investigation itself was just out of a sense of
duty as someone involved in the revolution, and a thirst for knowledge.”

“Good grief, like father like
daughter–you both like to traipse all over people’s business.”

My father continued speaking, not
responding to Germaine’s sarcasm. “As a result, it’s no longer just
someone else’s problem.”

“So you called us here hoping
to hear the whole story with all the involved parties together.”

Germaine had been able to guess the
direction the conversation was going too.

“Um…Papa, I’m sorry. For causing
you such trouble.”

When I said the apology that I had
been intending to make since yesterday, my father suddenly grew flustered.

“Wh-what-what are you saying?
It’s not your fault. It’s not your fault at all, Yukina, no. It’s all Kyle’s
fault, for becoming the yes-man to a demon.”

Upon realizing that Germaine was
watching our exchange with an unamused expression, Dad cleared his throat and
resumed his strong posture.

“Just to confirm.” He once
again turned to Germaine. “Is Kyle alright now?”

“You know that yourself to some
extent, don’t you?”

“What makes you say that?”

“If he wasn’t, you wouldn’t
have sent your son to Lucifenia.”

“…Most impressive.”

It sounded like my father had
grasped much more information than I’d thought.

In that case–

“Papa! There are two people
that we need you to tell us where they are.”

Dad looked somewhat surprised at my abruptly
cutting in.

After prefacing it with,
“Assuming I know,” he returned to his usual smile. “Fine. Ask Papa
anything.”

First was Ney Phatipe. When I
brought up her name, Dad responded without pause, “The head of the Special
Maneuvers Task Force, eh? She came into Marlon several days before the rest of
you, and was seen heading to Marlon Castle. After that, though, I don’t
know.”

So she was in Marlon after all.

“Marlon Castle…I wonder if
Kyle’s alright,” Germaine murmured, looking worried.

“Us aside, there’s no reason
for Ney to cause harm to King Kyle. It’s unthinkable that anyone would aim for
the king’s life inside a castle with so many other people in the first place.”

"I guess…Yeah.”

The anxiety vanished from Germaine’s
face.

Mister
Kyle and Miss Germaine…The two of them were so hostile with each other until
just a short while ago…

A fun, wild idea came to mind, but
it vanished for the moment. For now the conversation continued.

“As for the other one…it’s
Elluka Clockworker. I thought maybe she’d be in Marlon too.”

This time, a short silence ensued.
My father flipped through the documents he had on hand.

“She…” Finally he opened
his mouth. “She might be gone.”

“…! What are you saying?”

“Elluka and Gumillia met with
Abyss I.R. three years ago. Apparently at the time the two of them had been caught
in a trap, and were harshly attacked by Abyss and the Special Maneuvers Task
Force.”

“It can’t be…”

I couldn’t imagine that Gumillia and
her mentor Elluka would be defeated by her enemy so easily. Was this Abyss I.R.
really such a frightening sorceress?

“Gumillia somehow managed to
escape, but Elluka was captured.” He told us that after that he was
completely in the dark on her movements. “It’s best to be prepared for the
fact that she may already be dead.”

Germaine gazed at the Column forest
outside the window.

“…Maybe we shouldn’t say that
to Master Gumillia.”

My father clapped his hands twice,
as though to dispel the heavy air.

“Come, now it’s my turn.”
He slightly rose up from his chair. “Let me hear
everything you two have experienced.”

After that Germaine and I told him
of the events that had happened since I entered Beelzenia.

Taking some mild artistic licenses with
it so he wouldn’t faint, of course.

♣ Yukina ~The Country of Marlon, “Freezis Estate/Storeroom”~

Three rooms down from my father’s
room was a storeroom.

Though calling it a storeroom would make him
mad. To be accurate, it was a “museum” where my father’s collection
could be on display.

I just couldn’t understand that
collecting mania of his. It was just an inconvenience, gathering so much junk.
When I told my mother that, she’d just smiled and said, “You think that
because you’re a girl, Yukina.”

Anyway, that aside I was now
standing in front of the doorway to his “museum”–naturally I had a
